The bedside gap

You can see the crisis. You should not be left with nowhere to send it.

I had documented ADHD. I had addiction crisis. I had repeated emergency contact. I had hospital admissions, withdrawal, collapse and risk. The system kept seeing the fire and missing the driver underneath it.

The Alcohol Care Team sits at one of the most important points in that pathway: the moment when somebody is alive, visible, scared enough to listen, and close enough to being lost again.

OTOS is not asking Alcohol Care to diagnose ADHD or carry the next service’s risk. It is asking whether that bedside moment can create a continuity touchpoint: a simple record that the person was seen, the next step was offered, and the handoff did not vanish into silence.

Illustrative bedside handoff concept. Not live. Not an NHS product.

What I imagine testing

A light touchpoint. A visible next step. A receipt that it landed.

Touchpoint

A person from the 50-person cohort is seen by Alcohol Care. The agreed OTOS touchpoint is logged.

Next step

The person is pointed back into the agreed addiction / ADHD pathway. No new clinical responsibility moves to OTOS.

Receipt

The demonstrator can see whether the next step landed, stalled or disappeared. That is the proof we are trying to gather.

What I am asking

Not a rollout. Not paperwork. A 30-minute conversation to pressure-test the bedside route.

If this can be done without disruption, without extra clinical burden and without confusing responsibility, it could become one of the most valuable touchpoints in the whole demonstrator. If it cannot, we say so honestly and stop there.
