The shared ask

One named contact. One honest scoping conversation.

The demonstrator only works if each partner stays inside their real role. No pretend integration. No heroic workload. No made-up pathway. Just the smallest safe version of the truth.

The partner ecology

Clinical, addiction and holding routes all matter.

Alcohol Care, CGL/CRS and the ADHD Team are the primary pathway points. RCE and Mind / Jo Fenton matter because they show what post-medication support, attendance and safe charity touchpoints can add without becoming clinical services.

Partner routes

Choose the point where you already touch the journey.

The same demonstrator looks different depending on where you sit in the pathway. The pages below keep the ask specific, bounded and small.

  • Alcohol Care Team @ Addenbrooke’s: confirm a touchpoint if one of the agreed cohort presents.
  • CGL / CRS: help identify and hold the addiction-side pattern.
  • CPFT ADHD Team: help explore the medication-review and waiting-list risk question.
  • RCE Wellbeing Hub: course attendance and engagement can be positive continuity signals.
  • Mind / Jo Fenton: post-medication support can show what finally becomes usable when ADHD is treated.
  • GP / RTC / QbTech: potential ADHD access routes remain clinician-directed, not OTOS-directed.

What partner support means

A light signal is enough at this stage.

A short email or meeting agreement saying you are open to discussing the demonstrator gives the project enough legitimacy to move into funding and Local Authority scoping. It does not commit you to a rollout.
