The small thing that matters

Attendance is data. Not clinical data. Human continuity data.

I found RCE after medication had started and while I was still working out what OTOS was. That matters. It means I was not there as an unmedicated crisis referral. I was there as someone trying to rebuild.

But that is exactly why RCE belongs in the demonstrator. It is a real place people can go while the bigger clinical route is moving slowly. A course. A session. A reason to stay connected to life.

Stage 1 does not ask RCE to onboard new people. It asks whether a simple attendance acknowledgement can become one more signal that the person is still connected.

Everyday settings matter because recovery happens between appointments.

Future option

Micro check-ins could point to real courses, not empty advice.

If agreed later, OTOS SMS check-ins could include RCE course prompts. Even a delivered message is a signal. An opened link is stronger. Attendance is stronger again. None of that makes RCE clinical. It simply makes the holding work visible.
