The cohort question

Messaging and drift

The language matters. The nudge matters. The silence matters.

Generic reminders do not understand shame, avoidance, relapse, ADHD paralysis or the moment someone is close to disappearing. CGL knows that language better than anyone. I want those communications co-written with the people who actually understand the cohort.

Micro check-ins

Small, human, low-pressure messages. Not “comply with service.” More like: we are still here, and this is the next small step.

Digital footprint

Message delivered, phone active, link opened, reply sent, course attended. Tiny signals that someone has not vanished.

Human review

If drift appears, the agreed human reviewer sees it. OTOS does not decide. People decide.
