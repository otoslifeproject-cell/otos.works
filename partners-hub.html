02 · The shared ask

One named contact. One honest scoping conversation.

The next step is not a purchase decision or a rollout commitment. It is a short, direct pathway conversation to see whether the demonstrator is safe, bounded and useful.

Named contact

One person per organisation to answer practical workflow questions and confirm local red lines.

Human reviewer

If a drift signal appears, a person reviews it and decides what, if anything, happens next.

Scoping conversation

Thirty minutes to map the pathway, agree boundaries and challenge the assumptions.

04 · Cohort anchor

The same overlap, seen from different doors.

The demonstrator starts with people already visible to services, where unresolved ADHD may be driving repeated addiction crisis, disengagement, relapse or referral drift. The cohort is not invented by OTOS; it is identified with partners.

  • Addiction pathway sees relapse, silence and return.
  • ADHD pathway sees waiting pressure and unmet need.
  • Hospital sees crisis moments that may be the chance to start the thread.
  • Community and charity support see the human rebuilding that happens between appointments.

Boundaries

What OTOS is - and what it is not.

This remains a private partner briefing and pre-pilot demonstrator frame, not a live clinical service. The purpose is to make the route visible enough for partners to correct, test and safely scope.

OTOS does not

  • diagnose, prescribe or triage clinically
  • replace NHS, local authority, CGL, CRS, GP, charity or partner records
  • write into NHS systems or create hidden clinical responsibility
  • change how any partner service runs
  • turn modelling into guaranteed outcomes

OTOS does

  • log agreed non-clinical touchpoints
  • show whether handoffs landed, stalled or disappeared
  • surface drift signals for human review
  • hold a tokenised continuity thread
  • help partners test whether the ADHD-and-addiction overlap is visible locally
