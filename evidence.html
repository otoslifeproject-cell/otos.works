Published evidence

The wider direction is consistent.

Studies and guidance point to ADHD being clinically important in addiction, risk and broader outcomes. The demonstrator tests whether that direction is visible in this local pathway.

03

How to read this evidence

It supports a test, not a verdict.

The evidence is strong enough to justify a careful local demonstrator. It is not being used to claim OTOS already works, that partners are at fault, or that every addiction case is ADHD.

  • Published studies show the risk area is serious.
  • The protected clinical material makes the local question concrete.
  • CGL Nottingham exploratory work suggests the frontline question is already emerging elsewhere.

What happens next

The demonstrator replaces assumption with local proof.

The pre-pilot should answer a narrow question: can existing services safely recognise a small ADHD-and-addiction overlap cohort, log useful touchpoints, and learn whether continuity changes what happens next?

  • Keep source detail on the evidence sources page.
  • Keep figures caveated and source-labelled.
  • Use partner sense-check before any larger pilot claim.
