01 · Core limit

Potential ADHD access routes for clinical discussion.

The pre-pilot does not decide who gets assessed, prioritised or routed. It helps partners understand whether there is a recognisable ADHD-and-addiction cohort, then lets the ADHD Team and clinical partners define the safest route.

02 · Possible routes

Visible options, clinically governed.

Possible routes may include the existing CPFT pathway, Right to Choose advice, QbTest / QbTech support if clinically appropriate, and GP shared-care considerations. None of these are owned by OTOS.

  • Existing CPFT pathway.
  • Right to Choose advice.
  • QbTest / QbTech support if clinically appropriate.
  • GP shared-care considerations.

03 · Clear limits

OTOS does not instruct, prioritise or prescribe.

The demonstrator can make the route visible and receipted. It cannot instruct GPs, choose RTC providers, create clinical priority or hold prescribing responsibility.

Boundaries

What OTOS is - and what it is not.

This remains a private partner briefing and pre-pilot demonstrator frame, not a live clinical service. The purpose is to make the route visible enough for partners to correct, test and safely scope.

OTOS does not

  • diagnose, prescribe or triage clinically
  • replace NHS, local authority, CGL, CRS, GP, charity or partner records
  • write into NHS systems or create hidden clinical responsibility
  • change how any partner service runs
  • turn modelling into guaranteed outcomes

OTOS does

  • log agreed non-clinical touchpoints
  • show whether handoffs landed, stalled or disappeared
  • surface drift signals for human review
  • hold a tokenised continuity thread
  • help partners test whether the ADHD-and-addiction overlap is visible locally
