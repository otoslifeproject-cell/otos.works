Starter source set

Current named sources.

Protected clinical correspondence

Supports: case-specific clinical context for asking the ADHD/addiction continuity question.

Does not prove: OTOS outcomes, wider prevalence, or partner fault.

Reference: held privately for the right conversation.

START study

Supports: ADHD diagnosis/treatment relevance in people engaged with addiction-treatment populations.

Does not prove: that every addiction case is ADHD, or that OTOS should screen or diagnose.

Reference: add full citation/link during final evidence pack pass.

BMJ / Southampton–Karolinska target-trial emulation

Supports: ADHD medication being associated with lower rates of serious adverse outcomes in a large Swedish cohort.

Does not prove: causation for every individual, local service impact, or OTOS effectiveness.

Reference: source-labelled study notes in the evidence pack.

RCPsych CR235

Supports: adult ADHD good-practice context and the need for careful clinical ownership.

Does not prove: pathway performance in Cambridge and Peterborough or any specific demonstrator result.

Reference: formal guidance source for clinical boundary language.
