Local Authority · Public Health · financing route

A cost-redirection opportunity hiding in current spend.

The immediate goal is partner openness, because that gives the project enough legitimacy to discuss demonstrator funding and local proof with public-sector stakeholders.

View planView numbers
02

The funding reality

The demo still needs a build.

The pre-pilot is not “no system”. It needs a small Continuity build capable of handling the demonstrator safely. The current expectation is roughly £90k minimum and probably closer to £150k, depending on final scope.

Why not build first

Partner openness comes before spend.

The project should not be built speculatively and then pushed into services. Partner support helps justify the financing conversation before the build begins.

03

What Local Authority needs to know

This is a low-risk proof move.

The demonstrator is designed to be surgical: small cohort, clear boundaries, no procurement ask, no full onboarding, and enough evidence to decide whether a larger pilot is justified.

  • Partner interest first.
  • Costed demonstrator second.
  • Full pilot only if evidence and appetite support it.
