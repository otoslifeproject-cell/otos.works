CPFT ADHD Team · formal pathway page

Known to the system. Still waiting at the point of risk.

This page is more formal because we have not met in the same way as other partners. The ask is not accusation. It is a careful question about medication review, addiction risk and what happens while people wait.

02

The hard fact

The letter changes the conversation.

A consultant psychiatrist has now linked my inadequately managed ADHD symptoms with excess alcohol use in writing and asked for faster access to effective NHS ADHD treatment.

The question

What happens when ADHD review does not arrive in time?

I understand service pressure and long waits. The demonstrator asks whether a small, already-visible cohort can be reviewed as a pathway-risk pattern rather than as isolated service episodes.

03

The pre-pilot ask

Help test the risk question.

This is not a request for the ADHD Team to adopt OTOS. It is a request to discuss whether a small demonstrator could safely identify and track ADHD/addiction overlap while clinical decisions remain with the proper teams.

  • No assumption that every addiction case is ADHD.
  • No pressure to diagnose through OTOS.
  • A bounded way to see whether the pattern exists locally.
