Addenbrooke’s · Alcohol Care · bedside signal

One scan. One receipt. The thread does not disappear.

For the demonstrator, Helen or the bedside team should not be asked to carry a new pathway. If one of the agreed 50 presents for detox or alcohol-related care, the action is simply to confirm the touchpoint.

02

The demonstrator version

If one of the agreed cohort presents, confirm once.

The bedside action is deliberately tiny: scan a QR code or enter a participant number, confirm attendance, and send the signal into the Continuity thread.

What this is not

No new assessment. No extra clinical decision.

Alcohol Care is not being asked to diagnose ADHD, manage the wider pathway or carry a new service. The demonstrator only tests whether one simple signal changes what happens next.

03

The receipt

The whole point is proof that the touchpoint happened.

The screen should make the ask obvious: bedside attendance logged, receipt confirmed, ADHD Team and CGL/CRS notified. No further action needed unless partners agree otherwise.

  • Scan or enter participant number.
  • Confirm attendance.
  • Continuity sends the agreed signal.
