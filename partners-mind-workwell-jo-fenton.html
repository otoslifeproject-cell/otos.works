The community role

Not everything important is clinical.

The person between services still needs somewhere human to land. Charity and community support can be the contact that stops the thread snapping.

The specific point

The GP referral matters.

Being sent to Jo Fenton through a GP route shows how community support can sit in the real pathway, not as an add-on, but as part of how someone survives the gap.

What the demonstrator could learn

Which non-clinical supports help keep people connected?

The pre-pilot can record community support as a continuity touchpoint without asking charities to become clinical pathway owners.

  • A human landing place.
  • A signposting route.
  • A positive contact signal.
