Ripple effect

One person. Every life around them.

When ADHD-driven addiction goes undiagnosed, the cost is not contained to one person. It radiates outward - ring by ring - touching everyone around them, then everyone around them.

Read numbersBack to evidence

01 · Core thesis

The ripple is human before it is financial.

The closest people feel it first: partner, children, parents, siblings, friends and neighbours. Then services feel it: GP, ambulance, hospital, addiction, mental health, police, courts, housing and benefits.

02 · Model discipline

A ripple model is not a savings promise.

Use conservative costs, clear assumptions and visible caveats. The demonstrator does not claim to stop every ripple. It tests whether earlier continuity can reduce preventable escalation.

Show the rings clearly.

Boundaries

What OTOS is - and what it is not.

This remains a private partner briefing and pre-pilot demonstrator frame, not a live clinical service. The purpose is to make the route visible enough for partners to correct, test and safely scope.

OTOS does not

  • diagnose, prescribe or triage clinically
  • replace NHS, local authority, CGL, CRS, GP, charity or partner records
  • write into NHS systems or create hidden clinical responsibility
  • change how any partner service runs
  • turn modelling into guaranteed outcomes

OTOS does

  • log agreed non-clinical touchpoints
  • show whether handoffs landed, stalled or disappeared
  • surface drift signals for human review
  • hold a tokenised continuity thread
  • help partners test whether the ADHD-and-addiction overlap is visible locally
