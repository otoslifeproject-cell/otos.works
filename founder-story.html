Founder story

When ADHD is left waiting,

addiction steps in — and it does not come quietly.

Open partner routesRead the plan
02

The chaos pattern

Drinking worked before it nearly killed me.

That is the part people miss. Alcohol was not only bad behaviour. It was relief, focus, shutdown, noise reduction and escape — an untreated ADHD brain finding the only chemical tool it could reach.

What services saw

The system treated what was on fire.

GP contact, A&E, addiction services, rehab, crisis, family pressure and recovery support all saw parts of the collapse. What did not happen quickly enough was one joined-up thread connecting the documented ADHD to the addiction crisis while it still mattered.

03

The brutal truth

I lost almost everything before the pattern became clear.

I lost the house, the business, my stability and nearly my life. There was a suicide attempt. For years, addiction looked like the problem, but it was also the signal: untreated ADHD, no continuity and no joined-up route out.

  • This is not blame.
  • It is not melodrama.
  • It is the risk pattern my life was showing.

04

The turning point

Once the ADHD was treated, support finally started to land.

Therapy, rehab, CGL, family, RCE, Mind and Jo Fenton-style support all mattered. But the same support became usable in a different way once the brain underneath could actually hold it.

Before

support was offered, but could not always land

After medication

structure, courses and support became usable

The signal

attendance and engagement started to mean something

The lesson

sequence and continuity matter as much as intention

Image slot: images/founder-story-image2.png

Design from experience

The lived experience is not the sales pitch. It is the design specification.

Continuity, receipts, drift signals, handoffs and human review are not abstract features. They are the missing pieces I needed while moving between services — and the reason I believe this has to be tested properly for the next person.

06

Move back to action

I survived it. Now I want to test the gap safely.

I am not asking partners to accept my story as proof. I am asking whether we can test, carefully and locally, whether the same ADHD-and-addiction continuity gap is still swallowing other people.
