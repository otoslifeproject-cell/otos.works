Not a theory. A lived reality.

For thirty-five years, drinking worked before it nearly killed me. That is the part people miss. Alcohol was not just “bad behaviour”. It was relief, focus, shutdown, noise reduction, escape. It was an untreated ADHD brain finding the only chemical tool it could reach.

The system saw the consequences. GP. Police. A&E. ICU. Addiction services. Rehab. Probation. Mental health. What it did not do quickly enough was connect the documented ADHD to the addiction crisis while it still mattered.

When Elvanse finally landed, the addiction work finally had something stable to land on. That is the penny drop. That is why OTOS exists.

I am not asking anyone to take my story as clinical proof. I am asking partners to test whether the same pattern exists in others — safely, small, and with every boundary clear.
